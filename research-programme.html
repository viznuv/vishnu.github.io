Research Programme

A comprehensive empirical research portfolio employing rigorous experimental and computational methodologies to advance marketing theory and practice.

Marketing Technologies & AI-Driven Consumer Insights

AI Recommendation Systems

Compared personalised, collaborative-filter, and generic engines in field settings and found that context-aware hybrids deepened engagement and conversion quality. Multilevel mixed-effects and causal-inference estimators captured individual and situational heterogeneity across campaigns.

Generative AI for Personalisation

Built real-time fine-tuning workflows that aligned large language models with shopper intent, illustrating how conversational agents can deliver adaptive offers without compromising privacy. Evaluation studies positioned this framework as a scalable complement to classic recommenders.

Bayesian Marketing-Mix & Attribution

Developed PyMC-based models that reconstructed media decay, saturation, and interaction effects to estimate campaign effectiveness under uncertainty. A Shapley-inspired attribution pipeline reassigned conversion credit with impression-level precision, clarifying each channel's marginal contribution.

Virtual Consumption & Compulsive Buying

Triangulated survey experiments with behavioural traces to reveal how platform cues, fear of missing out, and reinforcement sensitivity shaped bursts of virtual spending. Structural-equation models showed impulsivity and perceived scarcity acting as sequential mediators of compulsive intent.

Brand Activism & Consumer Ethics

Trust Formation & Backlash Dynamics

Applied a brand-cause-consumer balance framework to show how congruence and message believability strengthened trust, whereas misalignment triggered reactance. Longitudinal analyses linked these perceptual shifts to loyalty trajectories and advocacy behaviours.

Expectation Violation & Recovery

Through a programme of laboratory, field, and event studies examined how timing, valence, and attribution of activism missteps unravelled brand trust. Follow-up work detailed recovery strategies that re-stabilised consumer sentiment and mitigated financial fallout.

Platform Governance & Autonomy

Investigated how punitive tactics such as credential crackdowns eroded perceived autonomy, while engagement-oriented nudges restored cooperative behaviour. Findings integrated social-exchange logic with reactance-control theory to offer actionable governance guidelines.

Ethical Digital Behaviour

Explored normative determinants of collaborative consumption, focusing on password sharing, data transparency, and pro-environmental purchasing. The research clarified how perceptions of fairness and self-determination shaped ethical compliance in digital contexts.

Digital Communication & Societal Dynamics

Credibility & Community Fact-Checking

Analysed community-driven fact-checking notes and showed how bipartisan wording and source diversity enhanced perceived helpfulness. Sentiment-trajectory mapping traced the diffusion of credibility cues throughout social networks.

Echo Chambers & Gatekeeping

Employed network clustering and Bayesian label propagation to detect emergent ideological clusters without imposing prior labels. Simulation studies indicated that moderation policy design influenced discourse diversity more than feed-ranking algorithms.

Attention Bias in Conversational Advertising

Reverse-engineered token-level attention patterns in chat models and demonstrated that strategically positioned brand keywords improved recall and persuasive intent. Controlled prompt-insertion tests validated the salience advantage of mid-answer placements for sponsored content.

Metaverse Service Perception

Extending the stereotype-content model, found that perceived warmth and competence jointly drove engagement and word-of-mouth in immersive retail and service contexts. Experimental results outlined actionable design levers for elevating virtual brand experiences.
