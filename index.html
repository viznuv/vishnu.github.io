Research

1. Marketing Technologies & AI-Driven Consumer Insights

Overview: Examines how data platforms and AI algorithms are reshaping marketing strategy and consumer decision-making. Highlights both experimental and modeling approaches to optimize relevance, engagement, and ROI.

  • AI Recommendation Systems: Compare personalized, collaborative, and generic engines to understand which algorithms drive the best consumer responses. Run field experiments and analyze results with multilevel mixed-effects models to capture individual and contextual effects.
  • Marketing Mix & Attribution Modeling: Build Bayesian MMMs in PyMC to estimate campaign ROI under uncertainty and cross-validation. Develop multi-touch attribution frameworks that integrate impression-level logs for precise channel contribution analysis.
  • Virtual Consumption & Compulsive Buying: Investigate compulsive buying in virtual environments via a multi-method blend of survey experiments and behavioral trace data. Employ structural equation modeling to test psychological mediators like impulsivity, FOMO, and reinforcement sensitivity.

2. Brand Activism & Consumer Ethics

Overview: Focuses on when and how brands take social or political stances—and the trust, backlash, or loyalty that follows. Combines experimental work with causal modeling and large-scale data analysis.

  • Trust Formation in Brand Activism: Manipulate cause relevance, hedonic framing, and message believability to see how they affect brand trust and purchase intent. Use structural equation models to test how consumer agreement with the cause moderates these relationships.
  • Ethical Digital Behavior: Explore determinants of password-sharing (e.g., Netflix case) through survey experiments and real-world usage data. Conduct meta-analyses of psychological drivers behind pro-environmental consumption in cause-related campaigns.
  • Financial Impact of Platform Strategies: Perform event-study and panel regressions to measure stock price and revenue reactions to punitive vs. engagement policies on sharing platforms. Theorize under a Social Exchange × Reactance-Control framework to link platform tactics with financial outcomes.

3. Social Media Dynamics & Polarization

Overview: Unpacks the socio-technical processes by which platforms drive echo chambers, credibility judgments, and engagement patterns. Emphasizes both qualitative frameworks and quantitative pipelines.

  • Social Media-Induced Polarization (SMIP): Conduct integrative reviews categorizing algorithmic filtering, affective polarization outcomes, and potential remedial cross-ideological cues. Design experiments that pair headline tone with celebrity endorsement cues to test dissonance effects.
  • Content Credibility & Community Trust: Apply NLP topic modeling and sentiment-trajectory analysis to Twitter Community Notes to map credibility signals over time. Build echo-chamber detection pipelines using network clustering and Bayesian inference to identify polarized clusters.
  • Moderation, Gatekeeping & Engagement: Develop a socio-technical framework combining Social Identity Complexity, affordance theory, and moral economy for hypothesis generation. Quantitatively analyze how shifts in moderation policies reshape user participation and discourse diversity.

Vision

Theoretical & Methodological Rationale

  • Interdisciplinary Synthesis: Integrate insights from marketing theory (e.g., attention economics, social exchange) with computational methods (e.g., machine learning, agent-based simulation) to generate testable hypotheses about consumer and social behavior.
  • Data-Driven Inquiry: Employ a full spectrum of data sources—from controlled experiments and surveys to large-scale digital trace data—applying advanced analytics (ML classification, causal inference, network analysis) to triangulate evidence and strengthen internal and external validity.
