Research Programme

A comprehensive portfolio of empirical research spanning AI-driven marketing, brand activism, and social media dynamics. Each study employs rigorous experimental and computational methods to advance both theory and practice.

Research Output by Area (2019-2024)

Figure 1: Research output distribution across primary areas (Publications & Working Papers)

AI Recommendation Systems

I compared personalised, collaborative-filter, and generic engines in field experiments, demonstrating that context-aware hybrids fostered richer consumer engagement. Multilevel mixed-effects and doubly-robust estimators captured individual and situational heterogeneity across campaigns.

Field Experiments Mixed-Effects Models Doubly-Robust Estimation

Bayesian Marketing-Mix & Attribution

I built PyMC-based models that reconstructed media decay, saturation, and interaction effects to estimate campaign ROI under uncertainty. A Shapley-inspired attribution pipeline reassigned conversion credit with impression-level precision, reducing channel overlap bias.

PyMC Models Shapley Attribution ROI Estimation

Virtual Consumption & Compulsive Buying

I triangulated survey experiments with behavioural-trace data to reveal how FOMO and reinforcement sensitivity triggered bursts of virtual spending. Structural-equation models confirmed impulsivity and perceived scarcity as sequential mediators of compulsive intent.

Survey Experiments Structural Equations Behavioral Traces

Trust & Backlash in Brand Activism

I manipulated cause relevance, hedonic framing, and message believability across multiple experiments, finding that congruent activism strengthened trust whereas mis-aligned stances provoked reactance. Event-study analyses linked these trust shifts to short-run market responses.

Experimental Design Event Studies Trust Modeling

Ethical Digital Behaviour

I examined Netflix password-sharing through survey experiments and usage logs, showing that perceived fairness and autonomy governed compliance more than formal sanctions. A broader meta-analysis mapped psychological antecedents of pro-environmental consumption motives in digital campaigns.

Meta-Analysis Usage Logs Compliance Studies

Platform Governance & Financial Impact

I tested a Social-Exchange × Reactance-Control framework with panel regressions on streaming platforms, revealing that punitive crackdowns depressed subscriber sentiment while engagement-oriented nudges restored momentum. The study integrated behavioural theory with finance-oriented event metrics.

Panel Regressions Social Exchange Theory Financial Metrics

Credibility & Community Fact-Checking

I mined Twitter Community Notes to demonstrate how bipartisan wording and source diversity enhanced perceived helpfulness. Topic-sentiment trajectories illustrated the diffusion of credibility cues through follower networks over time.

NLP Analysis Sentiment Trajectories Network Analysis

Echo Chambers & Gatekeeping

I deployed Bayesian label-propagation and network clustering to identify emergent ideological clusters without pre-labelling users. Simulation work suggested that moderation policies shifted discourse diversity more than algorithmic feed tweaks.

Bayesian Inference Network Clustering Simulation Studies

Human–LLM Opinion Dynamics

I analysed a large corpus of user–LLM dialogues and found that repeated exposure reduced lexical entropy yet widened belief distance on polarised topics. Sequential-linguistic divergence pointed to reinforcement mechanisms akin to echo-chamber drift.

Corpus Analysis Linguistic Divergence Opinion Dynamics

Attention-Bias Advertising in LLMs

I reverse-engineered token-level attention patterns in major chat models and showed that strategically positioned brand keywords heightened recall and intent in follow-up tasks. Controlled prompt-insertion experiments validated the salience advantage of mid-answer placements.

Attention Analysis Prompt Engineering Recall Studies

Vision & Values

My research philosophy centers on bridging theoretical rigor with practical impact, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration, and advancing responsible AI applications in marketing and social systems.

I aim to fuse behavioural theory with computational rigour so that every model translates into actionable uplift or risk diagnostics for practitioners. Future work will formalise this bridge through joint lab–industry consortia.

Actionable Insights Risk Diagnostics Industry Collaboration

Interdisciplinary Fusion

I routinely combine marketing, information-systems, and computer-science lenses, believing that multi-perspective triangulation yields more robust insights. Planned collaborations extend this synthesis to public-policy and ethics scholars.
